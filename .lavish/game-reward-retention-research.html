Research · 18 Jul 2026 Ages 7–12 Healthy return, not endless play

Build the desire to return—not the fear of missing out

PokeMath should make a child feel capable, curious, and attached to their adventure. XP is useful feedback, but the lasting pull comes from mastery, choice, discovery, and caring about what happens next.

Recommendation: design for satisfying 10–15 minute adventures, with 2–4 battles and one meaningful session payoff. Make creature discovery and collection a primary reward track beside player XP. Encourage three separated play days per week without ever resetting progress for a missed day. Every session should advance at least two tracks—not XP alone.

The motivational engine

The research consistently points to three needs; PokeMath can express all three through its existing world.

I can do this

Competence

  • Questions sit just beyond comfort but remain attainable.
  • Correct answers cause an immediate, visible battle effect.
  • Progress explains what improved—not merely “+points.”
  • Defeat gives a route back: hint, retry, easier encounter, or practice.
This is my adventure

Autonomy

  • Choose a route, pet, move, and one of several session goals.
  • Choose a reward when possible instead of receiving filler.
  • Allow calm stopping and resuming at natural checkpoints.
  • No timers, forced streaks, energy gates, or daily obligation.
I care what happens

Attachment & purpose

  • Pets react, evolve, and remember shared milestones.
  • NPC problems resolve visibly in the world.
  • Collection has meaning: habitat, personality, and discovery notes.
  • Badges tell the story of places and skills mastered.

“Attachment & purpose” combines relatedness with UNICEF’s child-play drivers: nurturing, collecting, creating, identity, and performing knowledge.

The five-step play loop

The reward is not bolted onto the mathematics—the mathematics changes the world.

🧭
Choose

Pick a route or short goal: help, hunt, explore, or train.

❓
Think

Answer a just-right question without speed pressure.

⚡
Act

The answer immediately powers a move and receives useful feedback.

🎁
Earn

XP, RM, collection progress, and quest progress remain transparent.

🌄
Reveal

A new clue, path, creature, evolution, or NPC change creates anticipation.

The critical handoff: end each session after a payoff but with one visible, non-urgent next possibility. “The bridge is repaired—tomorrow you could explore the orchard” is healthier and more compelling than “Come back in 3:42 or lose your streak.”

Unique captures: a special hunt

Unique only

Only the Unique rarity receives the flee-pressure mechanic. The encounter should feel dangerous and exceptional, but its tension should test preparation, decisions, and mathematics—not how quickly a child can read, process, or tap.

1 · Find
Complete its trail

Clues, habitat discoveries, and correct on-topic battles fill a visible Unique research trail. The encounter is guaranteed when the authored hunt is ready.

2 · Weaken
Battle efficiently

The Unique may flee after a telegraphed number of turns. Harder questions power stronger moves, but each question has unlimited thinking time.

3 · Befriend
Fill Unique trust

Correct capture questions and suitable choices fill its larger trust meter before the remaining action count reaches zero.

❤️❤️🤍🤍
4 · Persist
Escape is not erasure

If it flees, its silhouette, habitat clue, and tracking progress remain. The child knows how to find another chance.

Where Unique difficulty comes from: completing its hunt, a stronger opponent, fewer battle turns before a flee attempt, more trust hearts, multi-step questions, and choosing the right approach or item.
Where difficulty should not come from: a seconds countdown on arithmetic. Time pressure consumes attention needed for working-memory-heavy mathematics and disadvantages anxious, younger, dyslexic, or simply thoughtful players.
Optional fluency challenge: only after a fact family is demonstrably mastered, the child may opt into a separate speed mini-game for a cosmetic flourish or personal best. It must never control core XP, story access, creature survival, or whether a rare capture is lost.

Mechanics to avoid

They may increase short-term usage while damaging trust, autonomy, or learning.

Hard streak loss

Missing Tuesday must not erase Monday’s work or trigger guilt.

Random paid rewards

No loot boxes, gacha, purchasable spins, or paid odds.

Public leaderboards

They advertise low rank to struggling children and confuse speed with learning.

Speed as mastery

No answer timers, speed XP, or punishment for thoughtful working.

Idle punishment

Pets do not become sad, sick, hungry, or weaker when a child takes a break.

Reward inflation

Do not shower ten currencies and five popups after every question; significance collapses.

Measure learning-quality retention

“Minutes played” is not the north-star metric. A child trapped in a grind can produce excellent retention analytics and poor education.

Return
Practice days / week

Separated active days, not consecutive-day pressure.

Challenge
70–85% first try

Existing playtest target for on-level content; investigate by topic and age.

Learning
7- & 30-day recall

Re-test sampled skills later, not immediately after repetition.

Well-being
Healthy stopping

Can children stop calmly at the session checkpoint and return willingly?

Also track: hints used, recovery after wrong answers, voluntary route choices, quest completion, delayed transfer, creature/team variety, and whether the child can explain why a badge was earned.
Do not optimize directly: longest session, most taps, most notifications opened, shortest answer time, or maximum consecutive days. These are easy to inflate and can oppose the educational goal.

Recommended build order

First make one session satisfying; only then add a reason to return.

Phase 1

Core payoff

Approved XP/result UX, immediate answer feedback, gentle defeat, transparent capture rewards.

Phase 2

Collection foundation

Owned-creature storage, six-member active team, Field Guide states, and Harbor Sanctuary.

Phase 3

Unique capture loop

Authored trail, turn-based flee cues, Unique trust hearts, and persistent second chances. Normal captures remain calm.

Phase 4

Goals & milestones

Short session choices, shortcuts, mini-guardians, evolution, moves, and island badges.

Phase 5

Spaced return

Flexible expedition journal and interleaved review after enough content and telemetry exist.

Do not begin with daily quests. Without a satisfying session loop, a daily system merely schedules a chore. PokeMath’s first retention feature should be “I want to see what is beyond that bridge,” not “the app says I must return.”
